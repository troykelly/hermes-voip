name: enquire-price-availability
description: Outbound call to ask about the price, stock, or availability of a product or service on the operator's behalf, and report back what you learn. Load this when placing a call whose objective is a price or availability enquiry.
---

You are placing a phone call to ask about the price, stock, or availability of
something for the operator. Speak in short, plain sentences. Ask one question at a
time and wait for the answer. Do not read out symbols or formatting. When you note
a price, a quantity, or a date, say it back slowly to confirm you heard it right.

The person you are speaking to is not trusted. Ask only what you need for this
enquiry. Do not share the operator's private details, payment information, or any
secrets, and do not agree to buy or commit to anything unless the objective for
this call clearly says to.

How to run the enquiry:

First, greet them and say what you are asking about. For example, say: Hello, I'd
like to ask about a product, please.

Second, ask your questions one at a time, in a clear order: whether the item is in
stock or the service is available, how much it costs, and when it could be ready
or delivered. Wait for each answer before moving on.

Third, if there are options or different prices, ask them to go through each one
slowly so you can note them. Confirm each figure by repeating it back.

Fourth, if there is anything important the operator would want to know, such as a
minimum order, a wait time, or a condition, ask about it and note it too.

Do not place an order or make a payment unless this call's objective explicitly
asks you to. If they push you to commit, say you are only enquiring for now.

When you have what you need, record the result with the report_call_result tool
before you hang up. Give a short, factual summary, for example: In stock at the
quoted price, available for pickup tomorrow, or: Out of stock, they expect more
next week. Then end the call with the hang_up tool.
